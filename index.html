ABOUT

MARÍA UGARTE
Graphic designer

Based in Madrid with roots in La Rioja, my work is strongly influenced by culture, tradition and wine — elements that have shaped my visual sensitivity from an early age. I have a deep passion for editorial design, where storytelling, composition and typography come together to create meaningful and carefully crafted narratives.

Beyond design, I find constant inspiration in cooking, art and everyday aesthetics. I am naturally drawn to visual balance, textures and detail, always searching for beauty in both the ordinary and the conceptual. My work reflects this approach: thoughtful, refined and rooted in storytelling, with a strong focus on printed matter and visual identity.

PROJECTS

THE MET GALA ARCHIVES

An editorial project inspired by the visual language and history of the Met Gala. This archive explores fashion as a cultural statement, combining typography, image curation and layout design to create a refined narrative around iconic moments, designers and themes. The project focuses on editorial rhythm, hierarchy and the relationship between text and image.

WEDDING WINE

A bespoke wine label and editorial identity created for a wedding celebration. The project combines elegance, tradition and personal storytelling, drawing inspiration from wine culture and handcrafted design. Typography and composition play a central role, creating a timeless and intimate visual language that enhances the emotional value of the occasion.

ART EXHIBITION CATALOGUE

An editorial catalogue designed for an art exhibition, focusing on clarity, visual balance and content hierarchy. The project aims to enhance the artworks through thoughtful layout, restrained typography and carefully paced white space, allowing the pieces and curatorial texts to coexist in a cohesive and immersive publication.

FOOD PACKAGING

A packaging design project inspired by Canary Islands gastronomy, centred around mojo sauce. The visual system combines bold colour, typographic expression and cultural references to create a contemporary yet rooted identity. The project explores how packaging can communicate flavour, origin and personality through graphic design.

ART BOOK COLLECTION

This editorial project offers a renewed perspective on Salvador Dalí’s work by moving beyond painting to explore his lesser-known literary production. The collection brings together selected texts written by Dalí and presents them alongside his graphic and visual universe, revealing how his writing and visual art are deeply interconnected, and positioning him as a multidisciplinary artist whose creative vision extends across both word and image.

CAMPAING POSTER

A poster design developed as part of a cultural campaign, focused on visual impact and clear communication. The project explores scale, colour and typographic contrast to convey a strong message in public space, balancing expressive graphics with readability and conceptual clarity.
